# Neighborhoods
In order to talk about distance between objects we use neigborhoods.

## Defn.

Let $a \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\epsilon > 0$. Then the $\epsilon$-neigborhood of $a$ is the set:

$$
\begin{align}
V_{\epsilon}(a): 
& = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : |x-a| < \epsilon \} \\
& = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : -\epsilon < x-a < \epsilon \} \\ 
& = \{x \in \mathbb{R} : a-\epsilon < x < \epsilon+a \} \\ 
& = (a-\epsilon, a+\epsilon)
\end{align}
$$

Note $(a-\epsilon, a+\epsilon)$ is the set of points that are $\epsilon$-close to $a$

## Theorem

$$
\begin{align}
\text{Let } a \in \mathbb{R}\\ 
x \in V_{\epsilon}(a) \land \forall ~\epsilon, \epsilon > 0 \\
\implies x = a
\end{align}
$$

**Proof**
$$
\begin{align}
\text{Suppose: }& x \in V_{\epsilon}(a) \land \forall ~\epsilon, \epsilon > 0 \\
& \implies \forall ~\epsilon, |x-a| < \epsilon \\
& \implies |x-a| = 0 \\
& \implies x-a = 0 \\
& \implies x = a \\
& \end{align}
$$
