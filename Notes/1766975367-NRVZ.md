# Triangle Inequality
> Statement: $a,b \in \mathbb{R} \implies |a+b| \leq |a| + |b|$

**Proof**
$$
\begin{align}
-|a| & \leq a \leq |a| \\
-|b| & \leq b \leq |b| \\
-|a|-|b| & \leq a+b \leq |a| + |b| \\
-(|a| + |b|) & \leq a+b \leq |a| + |b| \\
-c & \leq a+b \leq c \\
& \implies |a+b| \leq c \\
& \implies |a+b| \leq  |a| + |b| \\
\end{align}
$$

_Why the name?_: 
* Similar statement for length of vectors

$$ ||\vec{a} + \vec{b}|| \leq ||\vec{a}|| + ||\vec{b}|| $$

## Common Technique

Maybe we know something about a third point $y$

$$
\begin{align}
\text{Let } x,a,y \in \mathbb{R} & ~|x-a| \\ 
& = |x-y+y-a| \\ 
& = |(x-y)+(y-a)| \leq |x-y| + |y-a| \\ 
\end{align}
$$

## Corollary: 
$$
\begin{align}
& a_1, a_2, a_3,...,a_n \in \mathbb{R} \\
& \implies |a_1 + a_2 + a_3 +... +a_n| \leq |a_1| + |a_2| + ... + |a_n| \\
\end{align}
$$
