# Algebraic Properties of $\mathbb{R}$
On the set $\mathbb{R}$ there are two binary operations, addition and multiplication. 

These operations contain the following properties: 

## Addition Properties
1. Communitive 
    * $a + b = b + a ~\forall ~a,b \in \mathbb{R}$
2. Associative 
    * $(a+b) + c = a + (b + c) ~\forall ~a,b,c \in \mathbb{R}$
3. Zero
    * $\exists 0 \in \mathbb{R} \ni a+0=a$
4. Existence of Negative Elements 
    * $\forall a \in \mathbb{R}, \exists (-a) \in \mathbb{R} \ni a + (-a) = 0 \land (-a) + a = 0$

## Multiplication Properties
1. Communitive 
    * $\forall a,b \in \mathbb{R}, a \cdot b = b \cdot a$
2. Associative
    * $\forall a,b,c \in \mathbb{R}, (a \cdot b) \cdot c = a \cdot (b \cdot c)$
3. Existance of Unit Element
    * $\exists 1 \in \mathbb{R} \neq 0 \ni a \cdot 1 = a$
4. Existance of Reiprocals 
    * $\forall a \in \mathbb{R}, \exists \dfrac{1}{a} \in \mathbb{R} \ni a \cdot \dfrac{1}{a} = 1$
5. Distributive Property 
    * $\forall a,b,c \in \mathbb{R}, a \cdot (b \cdot c) = (a \cdot b) + (a \cdot c)$
